Performance Diagnostics & Recovery

Amazon Robotics  ·  Product Launch Engineer  ·  2025

How I spotted a 5% performance decline that no dashboard flagged, traced it to its root cause, automated the detection pipeline, and recovered 2 units per hour of lost throughput — 3 weeks before the standard tooling would have noticed.

Situation

Robot station performance at one of our fulfillment sites began declining. Standard dashboards — the ones everyone relied on — showed nothing unusual. The decline was subtle but real, and it was costing throughput. The absence of an alert wasn't the same as the absence of a problem.

Task

Identify the root cause of the performance drop, build tooling to surface it faster in the future, and recover the lost output.

What I Did
  1. 01
    Spotted the signal others missed. By correlating station health data, configuration change logs, and operational metrics manually in Python, I identified a 5% performance decline that hadn't triggered any standard alerts. The signal was real — just invisible to the existing dashboards.
  2. 02
    Root-caused the issue. Traced the decline to a configuration change that had quietly degraded pick rates across a subset of stations. Cross-referenced with maintenance logs to confirm the correlation and rule out confounders.
  3. 03
    Built a diagnostic pipeline. Automated the data extraction and correlation process in Python. What previously required 5 hours of manual work each week now runs in 30 minutes — and flags anomalies that the standard dashboards miss.
  4. 04
    Drove the fix. Worked with engineering and ops to correct the configuration. Monitored recovery using the new pipeline to confirm that UPH returned to baseline — and stayed there.
Result
5h→30m Weekly Extraction
2 UPH Recovered
3 wks Earlier Than Dashboards

The diagnostic pipeline continues to run weekly. It's now the first place the team looks when performance trends deviate — before any alert fires.
