Station Health Visibility Platform

Amazon Robotics  ·  Product Launch Engineer  ·  2025

How I went from zero real-time visibility into station health to a dashboard used by 200+ people across 4 fulfillment sites — built in 2 days with a browser extension and no access to internal systems.

Situation

I joined Amazon Robotics as a Product Launch Engineer supporting 4 ToteASRS fulfillment sites. Each site runs hundreds of robot stations 24/7, and operations managers had no real-time visibility into which stations were healthy or degraded. The only way to check was manually — slow, error-prone, and reactive. By the time a problem was noticed, throughput had already been hit.

Task

Design and build a solution that gives operations and reliability teams instant visibility into station health across all sites, so they can make staffing and maintenance decisions before problems impact throughput.

What I Did
  1. 01
    Researched the problem space. Spent 2 months exploring solutions, including a hardware approach using physical light switches. Ruled it out as too complex and slow to scale across hundreds of stations per site.
  2. 02
    Found the right lever. Discovered Tampermonkey, a browser extension that lets you inject custom overlays into existing web interfaces. This meant I could build on top of the existing ops dashboard without touching any internal systems or requiring deployment access.
  3. 03
    Built the MVP in 2 days. Built a working overlay that reads live station data and displays health status in real time directly inside the ops interface — color-coded, filterable, and zero training required.
  4. 04
    Connected the backend. Wired the tool to a SharePoint list via REST APIs. When a station goes unhealthy, it updates SharePoint, triggers a Power Automate flow, pushes a Slack alert to the floor health team, and surfaces it on the dashboard overlay.
  5. 05
    Rolled out across sites. Worked with Operations and RME teams to refine the tool based on real usage. It was formally adopted for station health reporting across all 4 ToteASRS sites.
Result
200+ Daily Users
1,000+ Stations Tracked
4 Sites Live
↑ Formally Adopted

The tool is now the standard method for station health reporting across all 4 sites. Ops and RME teams can see the full health picture in seconds — not hours.
